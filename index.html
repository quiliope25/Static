A Masterpiece of Tropical Architecture

The Bahay Kubo is more than just a hut; it is a structural representation of Filipino values. Built with Bamboo and Nipa, its design allows for maximum airflow and flexibility against earthquakes and typhoons.

Sustainable Engineering

Passive Cooling

Wide windows and slatted bamboo floors allow the breeze to cool the home naturally.

Flood Resilience

Raised on heavy timber posts, keeping the living quarters dry during rainy seasons.

Local Materials

Utilizing renewable resources like rattan, cane, and palm leaves from the surrounding land.
